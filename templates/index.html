Informed Consent

TREATMENT TO BE DONE: I understand and consent to have any treatment done by the dentist after the procedure, risk, benefits & cost have been fully explained. These treatment include, but are not limited to, X-rays, cleaning, periodontal treatments, fillings, crowns, bridges, all types of extraction, root canals, and/or dentures, local anesthetics & surgical cases.

DRUGS & MEDICATIONS: I understand that antibiotics, analgesics, & other medications can cause allergic reactions like redness & swelling of tissue, pain, itching, vomiting, and/or anaphylactic shock.

CHANGES IN TREATMENT PLAN: I understand that during treatment, it may be necessary to change/add procedures because of condition found while working on the teeth that was not discovered during examination.

RADIOGRAPH: I understand that an x-ray or a radiograph maybe necessary as part of diagnostic aid to come up with tentative diagnosis of my dental problem.

REMOVAL OF TEETH: I understand that alternatives to tooth removal (root canal therapy, crowns & periodontal surgery etc.) & I completely understand that these alternatives, including their risk & benefits prior to authorizing the dentist to remove all the infections.

CROWNS (CAPS) & BRIDGES: Preparing a tooth may irritate the nerve tissue in the center of the tooth, leaving extra sensitive to heat, cold & pressure. I understand that sometimes it is not possible to match the color of natural teeth exactly with artificial teeth.

ENDODONTICS (ROOT CANAL): I understand there is no guarantee that a root canal treatment will save a tooth & that complications can occur from the treatment.

PERIODONTAL DISEASE: I understand that periodontal disease is a serious condition causing gum & bone inflammation and/or loss and that can lead eventually to the loss of my teeth.

FILLINGS: I understand that care must be exercised in chewing on filling, especially during the first 24 hours to avoid breakage. Sensitivity is common, but usually temporary.

DENTURES: I understand that wearing of dentures can be difficult. Sore spots, altered speech & difficulty in eating are common problems.

I understand that dentistry is not an exact science and that no dentist can properly guarantee accurate results all the time.
